Circles - An Integrated Math 2 Introduction

Circles are geometric objects within mathematics.

Note however that Circles are NOT ellipses. In circles, all points are equal in distance to the center, whereas ellipses are different distances.

Circle Terminology

Term Description
Center The fixed point where all radii meet, aka midpoint. Coordinate represented by (h, k)
Circumference The length of the circle. Essentially the perimeter of the circle.
Radius A line segment from the center of the circle to any endpoint on the circumference of the circle. Represented as r
Diameter A line segment whose endpoints lie on the ends of the circle. It passes through the center and is double the radius. The diameter is a special type of chord, and is the longest one. Represented as d
Chord A line segment whose endpoints lie on the circle.
Tangent A line in the plane of the circle that intersects the circle in exactly one point.
Secant A line that intersects a circle in two points.
Arc A portion of the circumference of the given circle.
Major Arc An arc greater than 180°. To name, use the endpoints and middle point of the Arc.
Minor Arc An arc less than 180°. Name using the endpoints of the Arc.
Semi-Circles Contains a diameter, which are the endpoints of the Semi-Circle. Forms a 180° angle.
