Trigonometry Introduction

Trigonometry is the branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between right-triangles and their sides and angles.

Trigonometric Functions - Overview

An easy way to remember the main 3 functions is using the mnemomic, SOHCAHTOA!
Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse
Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

The functions are also considered the trigonometric ratios for right-triangles.

Function Abbreviation Relationship & Description
Sine sin sin(θ) =
Opposite / Hypotenuse
 Sine is the ratio of opposite over hypotenuse.
Cosine cos cos(θ) =
Adjacent / Hypotenuse
 Cosine is the ratio of adjacent over hypotenuse.
Tangent tan tan(θ) =
Opposite / Adjacent
 Tangent is the ratio of opposite over adjacent.
Inverse Sine sin-1, asin, arcsin sin-1
Opposite / Hypotenuse
= θ  Inverse sine is the angle of the two sides.
Inverse Cosine cos-1, acos, arccos cos-1
Hypotenuse / Adjacent
= θ  Inverse cosine is the angle of the two sides.
Inverse Tangent tan-1, atan, arctan tan-1
Adjacent / Opposite
= θ  Inverse tangent is the angle of the two sides.
Secant sec sec(θ) =
Hypotenuse / Adjacent
 The secant value is equal to 1 divided by cos.
Cosecant csc csc(θ) =
Hypotenuse / Opposite
 The cosecant value is equal to 1 divided by sin.
Cotangent cot cot(θ) =
Adjacent / Opposite
 The cotangent value is equal to 1 divided by tan.

Sine, Cosine, and Tangent can be used to return the value of a side length as it uses the side length and angle measure to determine this. Likewise, the inverses of them take the side lengths and return the angle instead.
